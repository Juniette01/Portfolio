A Classroom in a Box

VR/AR

Objective

The objective of this project was to protoype an environment in virtual reality or augmented reality that would allow children wtih autism to experience their new classroom before visiting it in person.

 

Design Precedents

As part of our secondary research we looked at design precedents that we would be competing with or that would help make our product accessible to more people. We found the following:

Floreo is a supplementary method of teaching social and comunication skills for individuals with Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD).

Google Cardboard is a possible low cost alternative to other VR systems.

You are a social detective! is a book about that explains social skills in kid friendly language.

 

Concept

We wanted to create a virtual reality experience that prepares students to interact with peers and teachers before they enter the classroom.

Our initial goals for the experience were as follows:

  • Acknowledge teacher via eye contact and verbal responses
  • Complete an everyday activity of the classroom, such as putting their bookbag away
  • Acknowledge a peer and interact with eye contact and verbal responses
  • Experience the "end the school day" routine

 

After discussing our intial concept, we decided to simplify our idea and focus on three main tasks:

  • The student puts their bookbag in a cubby to begin the day
  • The student meets with the teacher and has a brief interaction
  • The student learns the different areas of the classroom (reading area, computer station, and bathroom)

Next Steps

Our next steps would be to:

  • Incorporate the actual teacher who will be teaching the class into the VR experience instead of a generic VR teacher. This way the student recognizes their teacher when they meet.
  • Create more scenarios in the classroom
  • Track emotional recognition
